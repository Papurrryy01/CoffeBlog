Coffee Bean Types

There are four main coffee bean types, each with its own character. Arabica is the world’s favorite, known for its smooth, sweet taste with notes of fruit and chocolate. Robusta packs nearly double the caffeine, delivering a bold, bitter kick often used in espresso blends. Rarer varieties like Liberica bring floral, fruity, and smoky tones, while Excelsa—a branch of Liberica—adds tart, complex flavors that deepen blends. Together, they create the diverse coffee experiences we enjoy every day.

Coffee Benefits

Coffee is more than just an energy boost—it’s loaded with antioxidants that help fight free radicals and support overall health. Studies show it can improve focus, memory, and mood, while also boosting physical performance thanks to its natural caffeine content. Regular coffee drinking has even been linked to a lower risk of certain diseases like type 2 diabetes, liver conditions, and some neurodegenerative disorders. And beyond the science, coffee offers a comforting ritual that brings people together and fuels creativity every day.
